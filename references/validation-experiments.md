# Validation Experiments

Use this reference to design small GTM experiments. Prefer experiments that produce decision evidence, not vanity metrics.

## Experiment Rules

- Use 14 days when the user needs fast direction.
- Use 30 days when the motion needs more cycles, such as content, outbound, or community.
- Define success and failure before the experiment starts.
- Capture buyer language, objections, alternatives, and willingness to pay.
- End with a decision: continue, pivot segment, change motion, change positioning, or collect more evidence.

## Experiment Types

### Buyer Interview Sprint

Best for: idea, prototype, unclear pain, unclear segment.

Plan:

- Identify 10 to 15 target buyers in one candidate segment.
- Ask about trigger, current workaround, urgency, budget, and alternatives.
- Do not pitch until the pain is understood.

Success signal:

- At least 5 conversations reveal the same urgent pain and current workaround.

Failure threshold:

- Buyers understand the idea but do not describe active pain or recent attempts to solve it.

Decision:

- Continue if pain and trigger repeat. Pivot segment if pain is weak or inconsistent.

### Concierge Sales Test

Best for: first revenue, founder-led sales, B2B workflow products.

Plan:

- Build a list of 30 to 50 target accounts.
- Send highly specific outreach tied to a trigger.
- Offer a manual demo, audit, or pilot.

Success signal:

- Meaningful reply rate, qualified meetings, or one paid/committed pilot.

Failure threshold:

- Replies show curiosity but no urgency, budget, or owner.

Decision:

- Continue if conversations progress to next steps. Revise segment or pain if replies are polite but non-committal.

### Landing Page Smoke Test

Best for: testing positioning, pain language, and channel traffic.

Plan:

- Create one page for one segment and one pain.
- Drive traffic from a specific source.
- Ask for a high-intent action: book a call, request access, join waitlist with reason.

Success signal:

- Qualified conversions with clear reason for interest.

Failure threshold:

- Clicks without qualified intent or signups without relevant pain.

Decision:

- Continue if the page attracts the right buyer language. Revise positioning if traffic is right but conversion is weak.

### Outbound Message Test

Best for: outbound-led motion validation.

Plan:

- Build a narrow list around one trigger.
- Test 2 to 3 message angles.
- Personalize by trigger, not superficial facts.

Success signal:

- Replies mention the pain, ask for details, or accept a meeting.

Failure threshold:

- No replies, irrelevant replies, or "not a priority" responses.

Decision:

- Continue if one angle creates qualified conversations. Rework buyer/trigger if all angles fail.

### Developer Activation Test

Best for: developer-led products.

Plan:

- Give 5 to 10 target developers the docs, demo, repo, or sandbox.
- Observe whether they reach first useful result.
- Collect friction points and missing proof.

Success signal:

- Users reach first value without extensive handholding and can explain what they would use it for.

Failure threshold:

- Users like the idea but cannot finish setup or cannot identify a real use case.

Decision:

- Improve activation before investing in broader GTM.

### Marketplace Listing Test

Best for: platform and app ecosystem products.

Plan:

- Compare top marketplace listings.
- Draft a focused listing for one use case.
- Test with target users or publish to a small platform if available.

Success signal:

- Users understand the use case quickly and compare it favorably to alternatives.

Failure threshold:

- Users cannot tell why it is different or who it is for.

Decision:

- Revise category, title, proof, and screenshots before scaling.
