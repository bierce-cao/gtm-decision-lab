# Beachhead Scoring Rubric

Use this reference to compare candidate segments. Score segments only as precisely as the evidence allows.

## Segment Specificity

A segment is scoreable when it includes at least two of:

- Buyer or user role
- Trigger situation
- Use case
- Company type, size, or vertical
- Existing alternative
- Urgency source

Examples:

- Weak: "B2B SaaS"
- Better: "Series A B2B SaaS RevOps teams struggling with manual pipeline hygiene before board reporting"
- Weak: "Developers"
- Better: "Open-source maintainers who need hosted analytics for project adoption reporting"

## Dimensions

Score 1 to 5. Add `[A]` beside any score based mainly on assumption.

### 1. Pain Intensity

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Pain is acute, repeated, and tied to revenue, compliance, trust, churn, or operational failure |
| 4 | Pain is clearly recognized and users have tried workarounds or alternatives |
| 3 | Pain is real but competes with other priorities |
| 2 | Pain exists but is background annoyance |
| 1 | Pain is theoretical or mostly invented by the product team |

### 2. Urgency

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Buyer needs a solution now due to deadline, mandate, incident, budget cycle, or external change |
| 4 | Buyer is actively searching or evaluating |
| 3 | Buyer agrees it matters but has no immediate deadline |
| 2 | Buyer may care later |
| 1 | No observable urgency |

### 3. Ability to Pay

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Clear budget, buyer authority, and measurable ROI |
| 4 | Budget likely exists and price resistance is manageable |
| 3 | Budget possible but requires justification |
| 2 | Budget unclear or controlled by another function |
| 1 | No budget or strong free-tool expectation |

### 4. Reachability

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Buyer can be reached directly through known lists, communities, events, marketplaces, or existing audience |
| 4 | Buyer is findable with targeted research and repeatable outreach |
| 3 | Buyer is reachable but acquisition cost or effort is uncertain |
| 2 | Buyer is fragmented or hidden behind gatekeepers |
| 1 | No clear path to reach buyers |

### 5. Winnability

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | No dominant incumbent, strong wedge, clear dissatisfaction with alternatives |
| 4 | Incumbents exist but have visible gaps the product can exploit |
| 3 | Can win some deals with effort, but dominance is uncertain |
| 2 | Entrenched alternatives, high switching costs, weak differentiation |
| 1 | Segment is effectively owned by incumbents or internal workflows |

### 6. Proof Speed

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Value can be proven in one meeting, demo, workflow, or within days |
| 4 | Value can be proven in a short pilot |
| 3 | Value proof takes weeks and depends on implementation |
| 2 | Value proof takes months or requires deep integration |
| 1 | Value proof is indirect or hard to attribute |

### 7. Reference Value

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | One customer can unlock many similar buyers through trust, community, or category visibility |
| 4 | Case studies travel well inside the segment |
| 3 | References help but do not create compounding access |
| 2 | Buyers are isolated or references are hard to use |
| 1 | References provide little leverage or customers compete with each other |

## Gates

Apply gates after scoring:

- Pain gate: pain intensity below 3 cannot be the beachhead.
- Urgency gate: urgency below 3 requires validation before GTM investment.
- Reachability gate: reachability below 3 means the segment may be real but not practical now.
- Assumption density gate: if 4 or more of 7 dimensions are assumptions, cap confidence at low.
- Winnability gate: winnability below 3 can be a future segment, not the first wedge.

## Recommendation Logic

Do not simply choose the highest total. Prefer the segment that combines:

- Pain at least 3
- Urgency at least 3
- Reachability at least 3
- Winnability at least 3
- Fastest path to proof
- Lowest number of critical assumptions

When two segments tie, prefer the one with stronger reachability and proof speed for early-stage users.

## Output Requirements

Always include:

- Recommended segment or hold decision
- Why this segment beats the alternatives
- Which assumptions could reverse the decision
- First validation step
- Deferred segments and revisit triggers
